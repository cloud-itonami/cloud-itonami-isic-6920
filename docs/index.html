Ledger-LLM ⊣ Audit Independence Governor

ISIC 6920 · maturity implemented

The problem

LLMs excel at drafting engagement summaries and normalizing intake, but they have no notion of which jurisdiction's professional-standards or independence requirements are official, no authority to issue real audit opinions or submit real tax filings, and no way to know whether an engagement carries undisclosed conflicts. Letting one issue an opinion directly invites fabricated citations, undisclosed conflicts, and wrong bookkeeping—and liability.

What you get

An independent Audit Independence Governor that holds unsafe commits—not LLM magic, but governed execution. The Governor wraps a sealed Ledger-LLM, enforces spec-cited professional standards, screens independence, and gates opinion issuance and tax filing (always human-approved). Immutable audit ledger records every decision.

What this is not

Not a hosted SaaS, not autonomous (opinion issuance and tax filing are never automatic), not a license to practice accounting in any jurisdiction (you supply that), and not a full materiality assessment. You supply jurisdiction-specific expertise and liability; this supplies the governed scaffold.
